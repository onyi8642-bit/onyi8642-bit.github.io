Reading 1 · reflection

I’m not really great at taking photos, so I’m not really into photography. I’m more into drawing and illustration. I'm a little unique, but I don't usually care about the story behind a photo or drawing; the piece should tell the story. Anything else I learn after the fact should add to it. An example is: I'm in class, we're analyzing a piece, I see nothing stands out, then the teacher talks about the backstory of the artist, or when it was made, then I go, "Ah, I see now." There's nothing bad about that, but for me, a picture should say a thousand words. I personally don’t think that I’ve gotten to that level of critical thought when it comes to my art, but that is my goal.

For most of my life, I tried to get as close to realism as possible in my art. I poured countless hours into perfecting the details and making my drawings as lifelike as I could. However, I've come to realize that it’s not just about replicating reality; it’s about capturing real emotion. What truly excites me is the challenge of illustrating the depth of human experiences and the complex realities of people's lives. I want my art to go beyond the surface and truly reflect the emotions that individuals experience in their daily journeys.

I love showing the reality of the lives people are living, the struggles, the joys, and the nuances that often go unnoticed. This passion extends beyond just my drawings; I envision this exploration of human emotion through various mediums such as books, shows, and short films. Each medium provides a unique opportunity to tell stories that resonate on a deep level, allowing viewers or readers to connect with the characters and their experiences.

In a singular piece, I aim to weave together layers of emotional depth and authenticity that captures a slice of life. Whether it’s a poignant moment between family members or a fleeting encounter with a stranger that sparks a profound realization, I want my audience to feel something. My goal is to evoke empathy and understanding, prompting individuals to reflect on their own feelings and experiences.

Through these creative expressions, I hope to foster connections and spark conversations about the shared human experience. It’s important for me to create art that not only represents reality but also inspires others to see and appreciate the beauty in the complexity of life. By striving for this emotional truth in all my work, I feel like I can contribute something meaningful to the world.

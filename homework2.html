Homework 2 · cropping & tonal range

Subject: Both images are taken from a third‑floor view of a New York City street — the fire escape, brick facades, and the quintessential city layers. I really liked the original color scheme of the first one (IMG_0283.PNG): the warm bricks, muted sky, and natural urban palette. After basic cropping (to emphasize the geometric lines of the buildings) and tonal adjustments (levels + subtle S‑curve), the image retained its authentic NYC mood.

Creative choice: For the second version (IMG_0284.PNG), I wanted to experiment with an inverted color scheme — not a simple negative, but a play on complementary shifts. The cool blues replace the warm bricks, and the sky turns amber. It transforms the familiar street into something surreal, almost like a dream version of the same view. While the original respects the documentary feel, the inverted one explores how color alone can redefine atmosphere. Both start from the same composition, but each tells a different emotional story.

📐 both images cropped to 1920×1080 px (RGB) · tonal adjustments via levels/curves · PSDs preserved.

Field Blur Exercise

Process: For this exercise, I started with a photograph where the entire scene was in focus. Using Photoshop's Field Blur tool (found in Filter > Blur Gallery > Field Blur), I created a shallow depth of field effect. The Field Blur tool allows you to place blur pins on different areas of the image and adjust the amount of blur independently for each pin.

Technique: I placed a blur pin on the subject I wanted to keep sharp and set the blur amount to 0. Then I added additional pins on the background elements and increased their blur values. The blur gradually transitions between pins, creating a natural depth effect. I also used the Bokeh controls to shape the quality of the blurred areas, adding a soft, lens-like appearance to the out-of-focus regions.

Result: The final image mimics what you would capture in-camera with a wide aperture lens. This technique is useful when you want to add depth and focus to a photo after it's been taken, or when you couldn't achieve the desired shallow depth of field during the shoot due to lighting or lens limitations.

Depth of Field Exercise

Shallow Depth of Field: This image uses a wide aperture (low f-stop) to create a shallow depth of field. The subject is sharp and isolated against a soft, blurred background. This technique draws attention to the main subject and creates a sense of intimacy. I used this approach to emphasize the details and textures of the foreground element while allowing the background to recede into soft shapes and colors.

Deep Depth of Field: For the second image, I used a narrow aperture (high f-stop) to achieve deep depth of field. Everything from the foreground to the background appears sharp and detailed. This technique works well for capturing expansive scenes where I want the viewer to explore every part of the composition. The deep focus allows the eye to travel through the frame, taking in the full context of the environment.

Both images were taken during our class shoot session. The shallow DOF shot was captured with an aperture of f/1.8, while the deep DOF shot used f/16. I wanted to show how the same scene can feel completely different depending on the aperture choice. The shallow focus creates a dreamy, focused mood, while the deep focus presents a more documentary-style perspective.
